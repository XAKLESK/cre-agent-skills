# Offer Negotiation and Closing Playbook Research

## Purpose

- Supports `knowledge/offer-negotiation-and-closing-playbook.md`
- Intended to create reusable seller-side guidance for bid comparison, BAFO, negotiation posture, PSA support, and diligence-to-close coordination

## U.S.-Only Assumptions

- Focus is seller-side U.S. investment sales brokerage
- The knowledge base should stay on the active transaction path from offers through closing
- Counsel and title handle legal interpretation and closing mechanics; the broker manages process and business issues

## Key Findings

- Seller-side brokers add the most value after offers arrive by:
  - normalizing terms
  - framing business tradeoffs
  - controlling BAFO
  - identifying retrade risk
  - coordinating diligence and closing milestones
- The playbook should treat post-offer execution as one continuous process:
  - compare bids
  - select or level bidders
  - negotiate key terms
  - move to signed paper
  - track diligence and closing until finish
- Legal-boundary sources strongly reinforce that the broker should summarize issues, not become substitute counsel.

## Benchmark and Process Decisions

- The knowledge base should include:
  - offer matrix fields
  - BAFO decision logic
  - seller-side negotiation priorities
  - backup-bid logic
  - diligence-to-close issue tracker categories
- It should clearly distinguish:
  - business decision support
  - legal review
  - title / escrow mechanics

## Conflicting Source Resolution

- Practitioner sale-process guides provide the most useful sequence for live deal handling.
- NAR handbook provisions govern listing-broker rights and offer presentation expectations.
- State-law and legal sources control on contract-drafting and legal-advice boundaries.

## Edge Cases and Red Flags

- Highest bid is not best bid because certainty is weak
- Broker allows the process to become bilateral negotiation without preserving leverage or backup options
- PSA support drifts into substantive legal drafting
- Diligence issues are being handled through scattered email with no owner or deadline

## Open Questions
