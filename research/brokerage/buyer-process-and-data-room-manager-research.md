# Buyer Process and Data Room Manager Research

## Purpose

- Supports `skills/brokerage/buyer-process-and-data-room-manager.md`
- Intended for seller-side U.S. investment sales brokers managing confidentiality, buyer qualification, data-room access, tours, and Q&A

## U.S.-Only Assumptions

- Focus is a seller-side commercial investment sales process in the United States
- Buyers may be institutions, private investors, 1031 exchange buyers, owner-users, or funds
- The process is controlled by the listing broker on behalf of the seller

## Key Findings

- Buyer process management is fundamentally about **controlled access plus buyer quality filtering**.
- Core controlled-process tasks:
  - prospect registration
  - confidentiality agreement execution
  - buyer qualification
  - OM / data-room release
  - tour management
  - question handling
  - seller updates
- Data-room access should be tiered where appropriate:
  - initial OM only
  - deeper data after CA and qualification
  - high-sensitivity material only for serious finalists when needed
- Buyer qualification should cover more than proof of funds:
  - decision maker identity
  - capital source
  - relevant track record
  - timeline credibility
  - financing and diligence expectations

## Benchmark and Process Decisions

- The skill should output:
  - buyer registration and qualification workflow
  - data-room access rules
  - tour protocol
  - RFI / Q&A tracking structure
  - seller update cadence
- The skill should explicitly distinguish:
  - interested prospect
  - qualified buyer
  - finalist buyer
- It should recommend documenting who received what and when, especially in managed processes.

## Conflicting Source Resolution

- Brokerage confidentiality forms differ, but all reinforce controlled sharing, restricted use, and buyer registration.
- Process guides vary in how much data is shared before offers, but all support more disciplined buyer qualification in higher-value or more confidential assignments.
- Platform marketing pages are helpful for workflow ideas but secondary to broker-controlled process artifacts.

## Edge Cases and Red Flags

- Buyers requesting tours or detailed data before signing CA
- Buyer appears capital-light, non-decision-making, or speculative but wants heavy process access
- Seller wants full transparency on buyer identity while also wanting maximum buyer count
- RFI process gets fragmented across email, calls, and tours with no single controlled log
